CHAPTER 1
Structure of the Cosmos

What is the structure of this creation? Where am I in this? Any world-view we subscribe to should answer this fundamental question, satisfying and matching with our experiences about this universe.

The most intriguing answers to this question is found in Sanatana Dharma, the closely knit set of ancient Indian philosophies. The most enthralling among them again, is the one declared by Lord Shree Krishna himself (who is one of the main manifestations of the God as per Hinduism) in his statement about reality in the renowned Bhagavad Gita. This precise statement was the answer to this question from his friend Arjuna, at the beginning of the great war in Mahabharata - “Dear Krishna, how is the perception of a self-realized person?”.

Shree Krishna’s answer to the above question forms the central concept of this book due to its potency to calibrate the world view even today - for the programmers and the builders of our modern world.

Now, hear what Shree Krishna told Arjuna on the structure of reality-

“The reality is structured as a huge tree, of which the root is at the top of the tree, while the branches are at the bottom of it. The leaves of this tree are the Vedic meters. A person who perceives this imperishable tree will be capable of understanding the whole essence of the eternal Vedas.”

So, why is this cryptic tree so important that the person who comprehends it can be considered as the knower of Vedas?

Let me rephrase the above in programmer-speak: “The reality is structured as a huge tree, of which the root node which is on the top of the tree, replicates itself downwards into similar children or branches, each being a smaller representation of itself. The leaf nodes of this fractal structure are the Vedic meters. A person who perceives this imperishable tree will be capable of understanding the nature of reality, and consequently the whole essence of the eternal Vedas.”

The Fractal Tree of Consciousness

What is a fractal ? Formally fractals are structures, each part of which has the same statistical character as the whole.

Fractals are found everywhere around us in nature – from micro crystal growth to the formation of Galaxies. But the most commonly perceived fractals by us are the trees. It's easy to notice that the main branches, the multiple levels of sub-branches, the twigs, all look like the tree itself.

This is the revelation from Lord Shri Krishna to the intuitives - This Universe is structured as a federated tree of consciousness, and that the supreme consciousness is the root node of this tree, which replicated into smaller constituent entities, all the way down to the leaf nodes. 

Each branch and twig of this tree are able to think and experience events by themselves in isolated contexts.

We can call them conscious nodes.

The leaves of this tree are the fundamental building blocks of the universe, which are the quantum reverberations (perceived by seers as Vedic meters).

The physical world, or Samsara - with space, time and everything within it, is a simulation for entertaining this immortal tree of consciousness.

Every node of the root tree takes an active part in this simulation, following more or less the same rules of simulation, also known as the Maya. And because space itself is an illusion within the Maya, and the tree being the original reality which runs it, it exists within a non-physical space of consciousness called Chidakasha.

The tree of consciousness is described as inverted for us to intuit that subsequent layers of fractals are derived from the parent layers preceding them. Preceding orders have higher conscious energy, or the will of actuation in the physical world. Within the Samsara, they also have a longer lifespan, higher mass and energy in their bodies. For example, we can see that our own bodies have much longer lifespans, mass and energy, compared to the tissues and cells within us.

The isolation of contexts, for registering and reacting to events in the Samsara as if each node is a separate entity, is made possible by applying the ternary properties called Gunas.

We will discuss what Gunas and Samsara are in subsequent posts.

For now, we can intuit that the infinite space, time, and entities within them which we can perceive with our senses, are part of this hard-to-break simulation. The knower and the knowledge is in fact, available right here, right now, within us.

CHAPTER 2
Tools of creation – The Gunas

So, if we are in reality, part of an immortal conscious structure beyond space and time, what exactly forms this structure ? What are the differentiating aspects which are required to form anything like a structure ? What makes me think that I’m an individual, in spite of me being an integral part of a larger system ?

If we, the conscious nodes, are beyond space and time, then what separates me from you? It has to be a layer of ignorance. Numerous pockets of awareness, interconnected with each other, can exist in a large framework built on ignorance. Remember, ignorance here means numbness at the most fundamental level of thinking, and not ignorance as it means in our daily lives.

Achieving isolation or inversely, propagation of information between these contexts (individual minds) which are numerous and distributed across multiple levels of The Tree, is important for the nodes to experience the events of Samsara as individuals, while still holding together as a self aware Universe.

For this, varying levels of ignorance and awareness (about the existence of higher and lower level contexts) need to be present in each of these nodes.

Additionally, there is also the intention to replicate a pattern, as an independent property at the most basic level. These three properties - awareness, ignorance and intention, which are the most fundamental building blocks of the meta-reality, are called Gunas.

Sattva Guna – The property of Awareness

The property of the conscious tree, by which a node would become aware of it being a part of its higher node and itself being made of numerous lower nodes, is called the Sattva Guna. The term refers to the property which is related to truth or knowing. Highest concentrations of Sattva Guna would make an entity an Avatar, meaning it would relate itself with the root node, and will be aware and empathetic to all nodes of the tree.

Tamo Guna – The property of Ignorance

Tamo guna provides isolation contexts for sub-nodes in the conscious tree by making a sub-node become unaware of it being a part of its parent node. High concentration of Tamo Guna would make a conscious node see itself as a completely independent entity with no empathy with its sibling nodes or parent nodes (at human levels, this would mean no empathy for other people or the environment). Further, Tamo Guna causes the absence of the feeling of responsibility and the concern about consequence of one’s actions.

Rajo Guna – The property of Intention

While a mix of Sat Guna and the Tamo Guna set the level of awareness and empathy of a conscious node, its actions using that state of awareness within the Samsara depend on how purposeful it is. Rajo Guna gives the energy of purpose to a conscious entity. Entities high in Rajo Guna, thus are agents of change within the Samsara. The character of the change they bring about, whether it’s good or bad for the system, depends on the mix of the first two gunas in them.

In the next chapter, we shall discuss how the three Gunas form the larger character and attitude of a conscious node or the individual soul.

Don’t worry, we shall see how.

The nodes where Green color is dominant will be an awareness dominated node, red zones mean the nodes there are hedonistic due to dominance of tamas and blue areas would mean purposeful entities due to dominance of Rajas.

The countless patterns thus formed by the properties of Sattva, Tamas and Rajas, can store tremendous amounts of information, readily perceivable to the conscious nodes through their tools of perception.

Turns out that we, and every other node in the immortal Tree, are provided such an information store, a sub-pattern, a node level context in “programmer-speak”, to record our individual experiences. This node level context is the higher character, called  Samskarah.

These patterns of samskaras, which form each of the pockets of consciousness -  the individual nodes or the individual souls - are so numerous that they are unique for each of these nodes.

Thus, it’s the unique samskaras that the souls have, which give a unique character to each of them.

CHAPTER 4
The Tools of Individual Souls - Buddhi, Manah and Indriyas

Now that we know that we are unique, immortal entities, enjoying the simulation of the physical universe, let’s now see what are the tools which we use to experience it.

Buddhi or the Intellect
The intellect can be thought of as the extended filaments, or the actuators of pure consciousness that reside within the framework of Gunas. It’s considered an actuator because of all the tools mentioned here, only Buddhi has the power to change samskaras of a soul.

Intellect allows a soul to introspect, dissect, discriminate, perceive the abstractions, and take right actions within the simulation of Samsara, which changes the patterns of samskara in its pocket within the great tree of consciousness.

Manah or the Mind

Mind or Manah is the essential “user experience” interface between the Soul and the Samsara, the simulated universe. Mind displays the three dimensional simulation, the numerous frames of Samsara driven on the time axis to fractal nodes of consciousness, the individual souls.

The perception given by Manas is dependent on the information gathered by its downstream sensory tools within Samsara, and the individual Samskara of the soul. This perception however can be subjected to correction using the Buddhi or intellect.

Mind is the essential interface between the ever existing soul and the ever changing samsara, the simulation. The downstream tools from here, namely the sensory interfaces, reside in, and define the building elements of Samsara.

Reside in, and define too, the individual, elementary aspects of the simulation.

Reside in, and define, elements of Samsara for the more abstract entities, the Manah and the Buddhi.

Indriyas or the Sensory Interfaces

The sensory interfaces emerging from the Mind or Manah are the following:

Abstract Ears or Shrotram, which defines the space and its property - the sound. Sound is the property of space - at the most abstract level. Space is the first element capable of carrying waves.

Abstract Touch Sense or Sparshanam, which defines the air and its property - of being felt, or the touch. Being felt is the property of air or Vayu - at the most abstract level. While Akasha is empty with respect to touchable entities, Vayu is the first touchable element.

Abstract Sight or Chakshu, which defines the Fire/Light and its property - of illuminating, or the being seen. Being seen is the property of fire or Agni - at the most abstract level. Agni is the first illuminating element.

Abstract Taste Sense or Rasanam, which defines the water and its property - of having a taste. Being tasted is the property of water or Jala - at the most abstract level. Water is the first solvent element, which is  the medium of taste.

Abstract Sense of Smell or Ghraanam, which defines the earth and its property - of having a distinct smell. Having a smell is the property of earth or Prithvi - at the most abstract level. Earth represents higher atomic (anu) elements and their associations which can be smelt.

Law of Karma

Earlier we saw that we are ever-existent fractals of conscious nodes gleefully animating physical bodies one after the other and enjoying the Samsara endlessly.

Given the fact that we are immortals, is there anything we have to care about ?

Yes, of course.

When we actively take part in Samsara through the body, we experience various types of emotions. A unit of emotion (Rasa) mostly falls into one of the following types –

  • Romance, Love, Lust, attractiveness
  • Laughter, mirth, comedy, bliss
  • Fury, Violence
  • Compassion, mercy, sadness
  • Disgust, aversion
  • Horror, terror
  • Courage, Daring, Heroism
  • Wonder, amazement
  • At peace

Sequence and duration of these feelings correspond to the event (Vibhava) we experience in Samsara.

This sequence of events we execute inside Samsara, which in turn creates in us various types of feelings, is orchestrated based on our past actions. This node-level context which stores impacts to other nodes by the actions of a node is called Karma.

If events caused by us were causes of joy for many other nodes, the events sequenced further for us to interact with, will be joyful too. Needless to say, each Rasa we propagate to other nodes, will eventually be fed back to us by Samsara. This is the ever strong Law of Karma.

CHAPTER 9

We now know that the events sequenced for execution by a conscious node is dependent on its past actions, as per the Law of Karma. This holds true at every level of the fractal tree.

If joyful events are in the sequence of execution for the majority of nodes which animates constituent cells of a physical body, the higher level node which animates that body also would have a sequence of blissful events in its life-pipeline. The inverse holds true too.

An example would be this : If majority of cells inside a body doesn’t follow the order prescribed for their cells, and destruction is pending for them as a result of their Karma back-log, the parent body of those cells would have a synchronous Karma back-log to justify its own discomfort when those cells get destroyed.

As every event at a lower level node has a bearing on the Rasa enjoyed by the parent/ancestor level nodes, if you had a way to know the Rasa enjoyed by a higher level node at future time, you can predict the events in the pipeline of lower level nodes too during that time.

From a human perspective, this means that – if you had a way to know the Rasa of planet-level entities that are strongly networked to you in the Samsara, you can very well predict the broader nature of events you will be experiencing too.

The Rasa experienced by Planets with respect to their mutual positioning in space was codified by humans with high property of awareness (Sattva Guna) and passed on to us, as Vedic Astrology or Jyotisha.

Jyothisha, which is part of the Vedas, is thus the high science involving mathematics and awareness about all of us being part of a unified simulation powered by the tree of consciousness.

  • Jyotisha classifies the entities born when the moon is in the rashis Aries (Mesha/Medam), Leo (Simha/Chingam) and Sagittarius (Dhanu) as Kshatriyas.

CHAPTER 12
Technology of Consciousness

There are means and ways, according to Sanatana Dharma, for conscious nodes at a particular level to communicate with nodes at higher levels to increase its influence within the Samsara. This is done by performing a precise set of actions from within the Samsara.

There are two branches practices for the purpose:

  • Vedachara  - The earliest way, directly recommended in the Vedic texts.
  • Agamas - Also known as Tantra, derived from the derivatives of the Vedas.

Every conscious node has a unique numerical pattern that resonates with it. This pattern depends on its position in the tree of consciousness. Other conscious nodes can communicate with it by invoking this numerical pattern in sequence and multiples.

Popular ways in Vedachara or Tantra to achieve numerical resonance and connect with a higher conscious node are by using rhythmic sounds – Mantras and by additionally using a representational geometry – Yantra, or in its advanced form, using Tantric materials and rituals.

Mantra

A Mantra contains sounds following a specific rhythm and pattern which help connect the entity chanting the mantra, to the higher level entity that Mantra resonates with. For example, Gayatri Mantra connects the chanter to the consciousness of the source of all energy of our world, the Sun.

Homas/Yagnas

Homas are Hindu Fire Rituals, which involves invoking the higher consciousness (Deity) into fire (Lord Agni) and offering the designated materials for that deity, into the fire. Homas make the higher entities invoked through them to be very receptive and affirmative to the doer of the Homas, and the influence of the will of the doer (on the Samsara) increases with this practice.

Yantra

Yantras are geometrical equivalents of Mantras. Yantras are used to capture the energy of Mantras for a specific purpose in a specific place. Focusing one’s mind on an empowered Yantra, has similar effects of chanting a mantra, or can expedite success when simultaneously chanting the corresponding Mantra.

Materials and Procedures

Advanced forms of Tantra also use specific materials and the prescribed procedures to use them. The mind, intellect and body of the practitioner are also active instruments in these procedures, which are believed to provide the practitioner the ability to manipulate the simulation-rules (Maya) of the Samsara at his/her will.

This knowledge of advanced Tantra is passed on from one generation to another by several low-key sects in India who usually keep themselves secluded and secretive (probably fearing abuse of these skills at the wrong hands.)

Japam

Japam is the repeated chanting of a Mantra that resonates with a higher node of consciousness, whose powers the person doing the japam desires to imbibe in. The number of repetitions in a session of Japam varies anywhere from 3 to 1000. Most people prefer 108 repetitions due to the overall significance of that number in Sanatana Dharma.

For example, it’s a common practice in India to chant the famous Gayatri Mantra 9 to 108 times as a morning ritual.

Vratam

Vratam is the practice of Japam with additional disciplines added with respect to food and sleep. For example, in the famous Ekadashi Vratam performed to get closer to the supreme consciousness, the performer of the Vratam abstains from food from sunrise of the day to the subsequent sunrise.

Tapas

Tapas is prolonged vratam which extends for many days or even years, during which solitude is generally preferred. A tapas is generally started with an intent (like attaining Moksha), and ends when the performer is certain that the intent is achieved.

Yoga & Asanas

Yoga refers to the union of an individual’s consciousness to that of a higher consciousness on which the individual is meditating upon when doing Japam, Vratam or Tapas. This is that state when the individual feels one with the higher consciousness, and is generally accompanied by feelings of bliss and independence.

During a prolonged Tapas, the ascetic performing it needs to change his/her body posture at regular intervals to enable better energy flow in the body, and also to avoid muscle wastage. The best of such postures facilitating better mind control and union with higher consciousness, were compiled by Indian sages as the Yoga Asanas.

A Note on Pran-Pratishtha and Temple worship

A very popular application of Tantra is Prana-Pratishta, which is the process of invoking and installing a higher node to reside in a lower level context of Samsara.

It follows the understanding that higher nodes can have multiple representational nodes in lower contexts. These representational nodes need to have strong connections established with the corresponding higher node.

The process invites higher consciousness into a representational body in a lower world in the Samsara, using Mantras. This is the main process followed to initiate Hindu temples. The idol or Murti of the higher node, is made from conforming materials in prescribed proportions. The scriptures are referred to when picking the Samsaric form of higher-node. The murti is then installed as a representational body of the higher consciousness using Tantric rituals. The temple premise is henceforth maintained according to the ways of the actual higher node itself. Also, the murti of the temple is henceforth addressed as Deity or Pratishtha of the temple.
